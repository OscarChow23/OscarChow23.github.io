About the Bayes factor

The Bayes factor quantifies how much more probable the observed NOvA data is under normal ordering than inverted ordering. A value greater than 1 favours normal ordering (blue); less than 1 favours inverted ordering (red). The strength of this preference depends critically on how precisely an external experiment — such as JUNO — constrains Δm²₃₂.
